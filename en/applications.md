# Applications

An **application** in weballoon is a saved shortcut to a website: a **name**, **URL**, icon, and options (workspace, notifications, proxy, protection, and so on).

## Adding an app from a URL

1. Open **My Apps**.
2. Click **Add Application**.
3. Enter **Application Name** and **Application URL**.
4. If you omit `http` or `https`, weballoon adds **`https://`** automatically.
5. Click **Save Changes**.

The new app is tied to the **workspace you are viewing** when you add it: either the **default** view (no workspace) or the named workspace selected in the workspace picker.

## Adding from the catalog

1. Open **Catalog**.
2. Use **Search** and category pills (**All** or a category name).
3. Click a tile. If the app is not already in the current workspace, you see **Get App**; after install the tile shows **In Workspace**.

Catalog entries install with the name, URL, and icon from the catalog.

## Opening and closing apps

- **Open:** Click the app in the **dock** or on the **My Apps** grid.
- **Close app view:** In the title bar, use **Close app** (X). This closes the embedded session view for that app; it does not delete the app from your list.

Navigation inside the app uses the title bar **Back**, **Home** (loads the app’s saved home URL), **Forward**, and **Reload**.

## Reordering apps

In the **dock**, drag one app icon onto another to **swap order**. The same order logic applies to the grid for the current workspace.

## Moving or removing apps

**Dock:** Right-click an app icon. You can move it to **Default View** or any workspace, or **Remove App** (with confirmation).

**My Apps grid:** Right-click a tile for the same move/remove options.

You **cannot** add the **same URL twice in the same workspace**; duplicate workspace options appear disabled with a “duplicated” note.

## Plan limits (Free)

On the **Free** plan, weballoon allows up to **10 applications** total and **2 named workspaces** (see [Plans & billing](plans-and-billing.md)). If you reach the app limit, **Add Application** is disabled and a banner offers to open **Plans** to upgrade.

## Favicons

Custom URL apps get an icon from a standard favicon service based on the site’s domain. Catalog apps use the catalog’s icon.
